Black Bean Fajita Casserole

A bold yet comforting black bean fajita bake with saucy peppers, tender corn tortillas, and salty cotija, finished fresh with avocado, sour cream, and cilantro.

___________________________________________________

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided

  • 2 medium red or orange bell peppers, sliced (about 4 cups)

  • 1 medium red onion (8 ounces), sliced (about 1½ cups)

  • 1 tablespoon less-sodium taco seasoning mix, divided

  • 6 medium cloves garlic, thinly sliced (about 2 tablespoons)

  • 1 (15-ounce) can no-salt-added black beans, rinsed

  • 1 cup refrigerated salsa

  • ½ cup water

  • ½ teaspoon salt

  • 5 (6-inch) white corn tortillas, halved and cut into 1-inch strips

  • 2 tablespoons crumbled cotija or feta cheese

  • 1 small ripe avocado, sliced (about ⅔ cup)

  • 2 tablespoons sour cream

  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro

  • Pickled jalapeño slices and/or hot sauce for serving (optional)

___________________________________________________

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F with rack in top third position. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a 12-inch cast-iron skillet over high heat until shimmering, about 30 seconds. Add sliced bell peppers and sliced red onion; cook, undisturbed, until charred on one side, about 6 minutes. Stir in 1½ teaspoons taco seasoning; cook, undisturbed, until the vegetables are softened and charred in spots, 2 to 3 minutes more. Transfer to a plate.

  2. Reduce heat to medium. Add sliced garlic and the remaining 1 tablespoon oil to the skillet; cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Stir in rinsed black beans, 1 cup salsa, ½ cup water, ½ teaspoon salt and the remaining 1½ teaspoons taco seasoning. Add the cooked vegetables and their juices, along with the tortilla strips. Stir until evenly coated. Remove from heat and sprinkle with 2 tablespoons cotija (or feta).

  3. Bake until bubbling around the edges and the cheese is melted, about 12 minutes.

  4. Top with sliced avocado, 2 tablespoons sour cream and 2 tablespoons cilantro. Garnish with pickled jalapeños and/or hot sauce, if desired.
